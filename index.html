About me

I was born in Kerala, India.

I completed my Bachelor's degree in 2011 from Mahatma Gandhi University, Kerala, India, in the field of Electronics and Communication. At the age of 22, I migrated to Germany to study Microsystems Engineering at the University of Freiburg. During my master studies, I worked as a research assistant in Fraunhofer ISE. My job centered around investigating the variation in the surface topology of large solar concentrators. I also worked as a teaching assistant in the Laboratory for the Design of Microsystems at IMTEK. In November 2013, I joined the Laboratory for Microactuators as a research assistant where I completed my master's degree with the thesis in "performance comparison and long-term stability of tunable lenses" in 2014. In July 2015 I started working on my PhD thesis at the Laboratory for Microactuators, where I work in a DFG funded project. My thesis deals with the development of adaptive mirror elements for the applications in the field of femtosecond lasers. For more details, check my research details in linkedin and publications.

While working on my PhD thesis, I also taught/assisted in undergrad lectures on Micro-actuators, Project management, and Sensors & Actuators. I also held the responsibilty for maintaining various equipments in the lab, including UV structuring laser, Laser profilometer, Microscopes, CNC Mill, Lathe, and also held the position as the network admin. Currently I am working as a process development Engineer at Robert Bosch GmbH.
